Guidelines for Queue Management

Overview

This process captures the objective, roles, responsibilities, and procedures that standardize Queue Management for Customer Support representatives. This process and document is for all Tiers of Customer Support so that each Tier is aware of the responsibilities and escalation guidelines for other Tiers.

Objective

The objective of this process is to set guidelines that allow Customer Support representatives to work their personal queues as productively and efficiently as possible throughout the business day. This process takes into consideration the following concepts of the daily workflow for Customer Support:

  • Tier – One of three Customer Support groups based on level of expertise.

  • Product – The application and/or service sold to the customers.

  • Escalated – A Customer Support representative is not able to resolve an issue and raises it to the next level of Support.

  • Priority – How important the issue is, based on the extent of its impact on the customer’s business.

Procedures

The steps and parameters detailed in this section are both procedural (in terms of Customer Support actions) and logical (in terms of automatic functionality built into Salesforce). However, this logic may not be included in Salesforce when we go live.

Salesforce automatically identifies and sorts cases based on the following rules:

  • Cases are organized within each analyst support queue.

  • Cases are organized within a Managed Services Queue with the ability to filter.

  • Anything identified as Production Down is automatically routed to Tier 2.
