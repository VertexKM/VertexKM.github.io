Product Application -- Tech Specialist Mentorship Program

Overview

The purpose of the Product Application – Tech Specialist Mentorship Program (the Mentorship Program) is to create an environment where Support SMEs and Support representatives work together in a coaching relationship to help Tier 1 and Tier 2 analysts improve their skills and to be successful in Customer Support.

Process

Customer Support assigns Mentors to new hires to provide guidance on troubleshooting issues and queue management as well as to increase knowledge of our products and Customer Support processes.

Mentorship Program

The Mentorship Program consists of two types of roles: Mentors and what Support refers to as Mentees.

Mentors are the senior support representatives providing guidance to newer team members.

Mentees are the newer support representatives receiving guidance.

In addition, the supervisors of both Mentors and Mentees play an active role in the Mentorship Program.

Selecting Mentors

Customer Support selects O Series Calc (Calc) and Compliance – Returns (Returns) SMEs based upon the selected criteria. Mentors must have or demonstrate:

  • Extensive product knowledge.

  • The Winning Way behaviors.

  • Successful partnerships in their role.

Benefits of being a Mentor

The benefits of being a mentor include:

  • Being off the phones for limited times or permanently.

  • Having reduced case intake with Toggle.

  • Career growth.

  • Career and leadership development.

Mentor Responsibilities

All Mentors are expected to:

  • Work with Mentees for six weeks (for details, see Mentorship Expectations and Goals), assisting them with troubleshooting issues they are assigned to by providing hints toward a solution.

  • Have daily check-ins.

  • Teach knowledge of the product and how to manage phone calls.

  • Provide helpful tips on managing daily responsibilities.

  • Provide honest, candid feedback to Mentees regarding their performance.

  • Reinforce Vertex Winning Way behavior.

  • Complete a weekly assessment (score card) and share it with the Mentee’s supervisor. This score card should evaluate the Mentee’s:

    • Product knowledge.

    • Issue assessment and progression to resolution.

    • Soft skills.

  • Review closed SRs. In reviewing closed SRs, Mentors should evaluate:

    • Whether each SR was correctly assessed.

    • Whether each SR was accurate, consistent, and timely.

Mentee Responsibilities

At the end of each week, Mentees share an end-of-week assessment with the supervisor of their Mentors. This assessment addresses the following Mentor-related questions:

  • Are they helpful?

  • Are they providing guidance?

  • Are they readily available?

  • Are they helping you to be successful? If not, what is needed?

Supervisor Responsibilities

At the end of each week, Mentors’ Supervisors are responsible for:

  • Holding weekly check-ins with Mentors.

  • Reviewing and discussing end-of-week reports that Mentors submit to their supervisors for each Customer Support analyst they are coaching.

  • Build roadmap to skill up developing performers.

  • Identify poor performers and (if there has been no improvement) discuss a path forward with Customer Support senior leadership.

Status of Mentorship Programs for each Support Area

As part of the Mentorship Program, Customer Support needs to identify and designate Tier 1 Product Application Specialists and Tier 2 Product Technical Specialists in their respective Support areas and products. These areas include:

  • Tier 1 O Series Calc

  • Tier 1 Compliance – Returns

  • Tier 2 O Series Calc

  • Tier 2 Compliance – Returns (We do not currently have a Mentorship Program for this tier.)

  • Tier 2 Foundation (STQ, STL, Commtax, Payroll) (We do not currently have a Mentorship Program for this tier.)

  • Tier 1 O Series Cloud Calc (BPO) is outsourced. Therefore, Customer Service does not offer a Mentorship program for this tier.

  • Tier 2 O Series Cloud Calc is currently merging with O Series Calc. To help get these transferred Customer Support analysts up-to-speed technically, Customer Support needs a Mentorship program for this tier though we do not currently have one.

Mentorship Expectations and Goals

Six-Week Plan for New Hires

The following table indicates the expectations and goals over a six-week period for Tier 1 Product Application Specialists and Tier 2 Product Technical Specialists involved in the Mentorship program.

Week

Expectations

Week 1

Assist in general product, internal tools, and process questions. Instill Winning Way behavior while Mentees work together in issue intake, assessment, and resolution.

Week 2

Continue knowledge sharing, review resolved cases and provide feedback to Mentees. Mentors review the Mentee’s overall performance and share their end-of-week assessment with their supervisor.

Week 3

Continue knowledge sharing. Answer specific questions and provide continuous feedback to Mentee. Share end-of-week assessment with supervisor.

Week 4

Mentees working their own case queue. Focus on quality and quantity of work. Share end-of-week assessment with supervisor.

Week 5

Mentees to listen to Mentors on customer calls, provide insight, best business practice, and tips on managing customers and issues. Share end-of-week assessment with supervisor.

Week 6

Mentors to listen to Mentees on customer calls. Continuous feedback to Mentee. Share end-of-week assessment with supervisor.
