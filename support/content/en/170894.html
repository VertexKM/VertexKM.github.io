Approved Customer Support Terminology

CRM: Process Terminology

The following list represents key terms that Customer Support uses in our processes. These terms support the larger goal of Standardization to Enable Growth.

Heading

Heading

Bug/Story

A product fix or enhancement that is put into a backlog for a future release.

Case

An issue (that is, Service Request) the customer submits to Customer Support.

Escalation

Engagement of a Supervisor or Manager in the resolution of an issue due to a customer or internally requested escalation.

Handoff

Sending a case, as a Request, to an External Service Group (such as, Tax Research, Product Management/CSE, Production Support, or Connector Development).

Prioritization

Defines the order by which an Analyst, specialist or external Service Group should address cases or requests. Prioritization is based on an assessment of the impact an issue is having on a customer’s business and the type of customer based on the customer’s profile.

Request

The mechanism by which Customer Support requests engagement by an external Service Group for case resolution (formerly known as a Request for Assistance – RFA).

Route

To transfer a Case or issue to another Tier within Customer Support using the Salesforce automated routing capability.

Skills Based Routing

Routing of a Case based on the level of product knowledge and other capabilities.

Tier

A Customer Support level to which Support specialist or consultant rout or transfer issues.

Transfer

To send a case to another Tier within Customer Support.

Salesforce Terminology

The followinglist represents key terms in Salesforce. A more comprehensive glossary of Salesforce terms is available online.

Heading

Heading

Account

An organization, company, or customer to be tracked (for example, a customer, partner, or competitor).

@Mention

An @mention (or at mention) is the @ symbol followed by a person’s or group’s name (for example, @Bob Smith or @DocTeam). You can @mention people in Chatter posts and Comments. When you @mention someone, the @mention links to their profile, the post or comment displays in their feed, and they are emailed a notification.

Case

Detailed information regarding a customer’s feedback, problem, or question. Used to track and solve customers’ issues.

Chatter Feed

A list of recent conversations, comments, and activities in Salesforce. Chatter feeds display:

  • On the Chatter or Home tab, where you can see your posts, posts from people you follow, and updates to records you follow, and posts to groups you're a member of.

  • On profiles, where you can see posts made by the person whose profile you're viewing.

  • On records, where you can see updates to the record you're viewing.

  • On Chatter groups, where you can see posts to the group you're viewing.

Community

Communities are customizable public or private spaces for employees, customers, and partners to collaborate on best practices and business processes.

Contacts

Contacts are the approved Administrators associated with an account.

Dashboard

A Dashboard is a visual display of data from source reports showing visual components, which can be charts, gauges, tables, metrics, or Visualforce pages. The components provide a snapshot of key metrics and performance indicators for your organization. Each dashboard can have up to 20 components.

Global Search

A Global Search searches more records and fields in Salesforce from the header search box. Global search keeps track of which objects you use and how often you use them and arranges the search results accordingly. Search results for the objects you use most frequently appear at the top of the list.

Home Tab

Starting page from which users can choose sidebar shortcuts and options, view current tasks and activities, or select another tab.

List View

A List View is a display of items (for example, Accounts or Contacts) based on specific criteria. Salesforce provides some predefined list views.

Message, Chatter

A Chatter Message is a private communication with other Chatter users. Use messages to send your manager a question privately or to communicate with a few select people when a discussion isn't relevant to everyone you work with. Messages don't appear in your feed, your profile, or any other part of Chatter that's publicly visible.

Owner

An Owner is the individual user to which a record (for example, a contact or case) is assigned.

Picklist

A Picklist is a selection list of options available for specific fields in a Salesforce object. For example, the Industry field for Accounts. Users can choose a value from the drop-down list of values or type the first few letters of a list item to select the value from a list of options rather than make an entry directly in the field.

Pinned Lists

Lists that display at the top or on the left side of a Service Cloud console. They're always visible and let you see the list you're working from and a record's details at the same time. The Pinned Lists feature must be turned on by an administrator.

Post

A top-level comment in a Chatter feed.

Profile

Defines a user’s permission to perform different functions within Salesforce. For example, the Solution Manager profile gives a user access to create, edit, and delete solutions.

Queue

A Queue is a holding area for items before they are processed. Salesforce uses queues in a number of different features and technologies.

Recent Activity

Recent Activity contains a summary of all the recent activity relating to components of the Service Console. For example, the Recent Activity page lists all the ideas and comments you have posted to a zone as well as the ideas you have voted on. You can view your Recent Activity page by clicking your nickname located on the right side of the Ideas tab.

Recent Items

Recent Items is a list of links in the sidebar for most recently accessed records. Note that not all types of records are listed in Recent Items.

Record

A Record is a single instance of a Salesforce object. For example, “John Jones” would be one instance of a Contact record.

Related List

The Related List is a section of a Record or other Detail Page that lists items related to that Record. For example, the Stage History Related List of an opportunity or the Open Activities related list of a case.

Relationship

A Relationship is a connection between two objects used to create Related Lists in Page Layouts and detail levels in Reports. Matching values in a specific field in both objects links related data. For example, if one object stores data about companies and another object stores data about people, a relationship allows you to find out which people work at the company.

Report

A Report returns a set of records that meets certain criteria and displays it in organized rows and columns. Report data can be filtered, grouped, and displayed graphically as a chart. Reports are stored in folders, which control who has access.

Role

A Role is the assigned responsibility of a user, partner account, or contact for specific accounts and Opportunities. Administrators can define user roles in Setup. Individual users can assign specific partner and contact roles for Accounts and Contacts.
