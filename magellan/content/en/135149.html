Writing a troubleshooting knowledge article

A troubleshooting article is designed to provide information about an issue and how a user can resolve or avoid the issue. Troubleshooting articles include the following information:

  • Problem - A summary of the problem that includes the symptoms, error messages, and possible triggers.

  • Cause - A description of why the problem occurs.

  • Solution - Information about how the user can correct the issue. If there is no permanent solution, include information on workarounds or ways to avoid the issue.

    Note: If your solution includes a workaround, you must review your approach with your manager.

When you write a troubleshooting article, consider the following design elements:

Element

Description

Title

Include a description of the problem from the customer's point of view.

Body

Includes a summary description of the problem, a description of the cause (if one is known), and the solution.

Procedure

Can include simple steps, but a troubleshooting article is usually not a good fit for a long procedure. Consider using a How-to article for that type of task.

Sections

Heading: Use headings with simple descriptors, to designate the different areas of the issue. Use the Heading 2 and Heading 3 styles for section headings.

Body: You may have multiple sub-sections grouped around a single subject area. The writer must make the determination on how to apply the groupings.
