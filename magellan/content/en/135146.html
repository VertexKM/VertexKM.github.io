Writing a how-to knowledge article

A how-to article is designed to inform the user about the steps needed to complete a specific task or perform a certain function. How-to articles typically include overview information and procedures.

To keep the knowledge article short, consider creating a separate document (short doc or User Guide) and adapt the knowledge article to provide overview information and a link to the document. Another option is to create multiple how-to articles with a main article that includes links to the related articles.

When you write a how-to article, consider the following design elements:

Element

Description

Title

A summary of the task to be performed. Do not overuse the title "how to" in multiple knowledge articles. Use words that represent the user's needs and actions, such as Configuring, Viewing, and Importing.

Body

Includes a summary description of the task or solution and graphics (if applicable).

Procedure

Can include longer procedure steps than a troubleshooting article, however, remember to follow the 7+/-2 rule for writing procedures.

Sections

Heading: Use headings with basic descriptors to designate the different areas of the issue. Use the Heading 2 and Heading 3 styles for section headings. Don't use a heading level below Heading 3.

Body: You may have multiple sub-sections grouped around a single subject area. The how-to article should have a single procedure.
