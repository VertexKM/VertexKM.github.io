Writing a Frequently Asked Question (FAQ) knowledge article

An FAQ article is designed to provide the user with answers to questions they may have. Questions can include general information about a topic or product-specific information.

An FAQ article may feature a single question and answer or multiple questions and answers around a central theme. If the article includes multiple questions, consider including a list of the questions at the beginning of the article with anchor links that take the user to the specific question.

When you write an FAQ article, consider the following design elements:

Element

Description

Title

A description of the central theme or subject area

Body

Includes a summary description of the questions and answers. Use the following guidelines:

  • Add the most popular or important questions at the top of the article.

  • Keep the answers brief and clear

  • Don't include too many questions, or no one will read your answers

Procedure

Can include simple processes or steps, but an FAQ article is usually not a good fit for a long procedure. Consider using a How-to article for that type of task.

Sections

Heading: Use a question format. Use the Heading 2 and Heading 3 styles for section headings.

Body: Use an answer format, which answers the question in the heading.
